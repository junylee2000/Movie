Grand Budapest Hotel (2014)

Director: Wes Anderson

Screenplay: Wes Anderson

The movie starts by going through layers of platforms to get to the main story as if we are going through the relics of the past to reach the story. In the movie, Zero, the bell boy and later the owner of the hotel, describe Mr. Gustave, the concierge of the hotel, as a person who “certainly sustained the illusion with a marvelous grace,” although his world disappeared a long time ago, even before he entered it. He explains that Mr. Gustave showed humanity in an inhumane world since the movie is set during the period of war. The movie goes through multiple layers to say that these nostalgia and romance in the past are only reachable through media like film and books.

"The Nostalgia that can only be experienced through multiple layers." -4/7/21

★★★★★
